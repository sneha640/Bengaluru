1.Lalbagh Botanical Garden

Lalbagh Botanical Garden, or shortened Lalbagh, is an old botanical garden in Bengaluru, India. First planned and laid out during the dalavaiship of Hyder Ali and later adorned with unique plant species by his son Tippu, it was later managed under numerous British Superintendents before Indian Independence. Lalbagh Botanical Garden is nationally and internationally renowned centre for botanical artwork, scientific study of plants and also conservation of plants. A heaven for all nature lovers, Lal Bagh covers an area 240 acres in the heart of the city and has nearly 1,854 species of plants.The garden features rare plants of French, Persian and Afghani origin and has attained the status of a Government Botanical Garden. The Lal Bagh Rock which is over 3000 million years old is found here and is a major tourist attraction. This botanical garden, a delight for photographers, also consists of the famous glass house where an annual flower show is held every year and is also a home to an aquarium and a lake,today Lalbagh Botanical Garden has one of the world's largest collection of rare plants.


Area: 97 ha
Founder: Hyder Ali
Metro: Bengaluru
District: Bengaluru Urban
